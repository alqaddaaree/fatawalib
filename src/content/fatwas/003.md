## Question

What is the ruling on narrating weak hadith for the purposes of encouragement and warnings (targhīb and tarhīb)?

## Answer

Narrating weak hadith for targhīb and tarhīb is not permissible in my view, contrary to what many scholars have permitted. This is because the condition they set — that one must clarify the weakness of the hadith — is rarely observed in practice. Most people who cite weak hadith for encouragement do not clarify their weakness, and so listeners assume they are established narrations from the Prophet ﷺ.

The Prophet ﷺ said: *"Lying about me is not like lying about anyone else. Whoever lies about me intentionally, let him take his seat in the Hellfire."* This is a severe warning against attributing to the Prophet ﷺ anything that is not authentically established from him.

The Qurān and the authentic Sunnah contain sufficient encouragement and warning without needing to resort to weak narrations. Whoever seeks to encourage people toward good or warn them from evil has an abundance of authentic texts at his disposal.

And Allāh knows best.
