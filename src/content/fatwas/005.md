question: "What is the ruling on working in a company that sells both permissible and impermissible goods?"
answer: |
  If the impermissible goods constitute only a small and incidental part of what the company sells, and the employee's work is specifically connected to the permissible goods, then there is leeway in this according to the opinion of a number of scholars.

  However, if the impermissible goods form a significant portion of the company's trade, or if the employee's work directly assists in the sale of impermissible items — such as being employed specifically to sell those items or to manage their distribution — then it is not permissible to work there. This is because the Prophet ﷺ cursed those who assist in the consumption of wine, including the one who carries it, the one who pours it, and the one who sells it.

  The Muslim should seek halal earnings and should not make excuses for himself in matters that involve clear assistance in sin. May Allāh provide for us all from lawful means.
